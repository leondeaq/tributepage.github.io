Elon Musk

The man who does the impossible

Here's a time line of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1971 - Born on June 28 in Pretoria, South Africa.
  • 1983 - At age 12, sells his first software, a video game called "Blastar," for approximately $500.
  • 1989 - Moves to Canada to attend Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario.
  • 1992 - Transfers to the University of Pennsylvania, where he earns a bachelor's degree in physics and economics.
  • 1995 - Founds his first company, Zip2, a software company providing city guide services to newspapers.
  • 1999 - Compaq buys Zip2 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options.
  • 1999 - Founds X.com, an online financial services and email payment company.
  • 2000 - X.com merges with Confinity, which has a money transfer service called PayPal.
  • 2002 - eBay buys PayPal for $1.5 billion in stock.
  • 2002 - Founds Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and facilitating the colonization of Mars.
  • 2004 - Joins Tesla Motors (now Tesla, Inc.) as chairman of the board and makes a significant investment in the company.
  • 2006 - Co-founds SolarCity, a solar energy services company.
  • 2008 - SpaceX launches Falcon 1, the first privately developed rocket to reach orbit.
  • 2008 - Becomes CEO of Tesla.
  • 2010 - Tesla Motors goes public, raising $226 million.
  • 2012 - SpaceX's Dragon capsule becomes the first commercial spacecraft to dock with the International Space Station (ISS).
  • 2013 - Publishes a concept paper for a high-speed transportation system called Hyperloop.
  • 2015 - Co-founds OpenAI, an artificial intelligence research company.
  • 2015 - SpaceX successfully lands the first Falcon 9 rocket on a ground-based platform.
  • 2016 - Founds Neuralink, a company focused on developing brain-computer interfaces.
  • 2016 - Establishes The Boring Company, a tunnel and infrastructure construction company.
  • 2017 - Tesla acquires SolarCity, integrating solar energy with energy storage products.
  • 2017 - Unveils the Tesla Semi truck and the new Roadster.
  • 2018 - SpaceX successfully launches the Falcon Heavy, the most powerful rocket in the world, with a Tesla Roadster as payload.
  • 2018 - Tesla reaches the goal of producing 5,000 Model 3 cars per week.
  • 2019 - SpaceX unveils the prototype of Starship, its spacecraft for missions to Mars.
  • 2019 - Tesla unveils the Cybertruck, an electric pickup truck.
  • 2020 - SpaceX launches the Crew Dragon Demo-2 mission, sending astronauts to the ISS and marking the first manned launch from U.S. soil in nearly a decade.
  • 2020 - Tesla is included in the S&P 500 index.
  • 2021 - Tesla reaches a market valuation of $1 trillion.
  • 2021 - Neuralink successfully tests its brain-computer interface on pigs and primates.
  • 2022 - SpaceX significantly expands the Starlink satellite constellation, offering global internet services.
  • 2022 - Elon Musk acquires Twitter for $44 billion.
  • 2023 - Tesla advances the development of its humanoid robot, Optimus.
  • 2023 - SpaceX continues significant testing of its Starship rocket, approaching long-duration flights.
  • 2024 - SpaceX is expected to conduct commercial flights with Starship.
  • 2024 - Significant advancements in human clinical trials of Neuralink are anticipated.

"When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor."

-- Elon Musk, CEO of Tesla
